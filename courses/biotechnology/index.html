Masters in Biotechnology in Ireland

Biotechnology is defined as the use of biological organisms/systems and processes for practical or commercial purposes.Biotechnology refers to the use of microorganisms or biological substances to develop new products of industrial, agricultural, or therapeutic interest to improve the quality of human life.Using the techniques of molecular biology, Msc in Biotechnology in Ireland will help you seek to understand and manipulate the basic building blocks of living things.

MSc in Biotechnology in Ireland graduates could expect to lead a research project and team or as a lab manager or teacher. Working in areas as diverse as food and agriculture, environmental conservation and medicine, a biotechnologist uses biological organisms to create and improve products and processes.Master's in Biotechnology in Ireland will help you gain a detailed technical understanding of the key advanced methods used in the contemporary biotechnology sector;

and an appreciation of how these techniques are applied both in biotechnology and in advanced research.

Best Universities in Ireland to Study Masters in Biotechnology:

  • University College Dublin
  • University of Limerick
  • National University of Ireland Galway
  • University College Cork
  • Dundalk Institute of Technology

Typical Academic Score in Undergraduate Degree and English Language Requirement :

  • Universities: 65% or 6.5 CGPA (on 10) and IELTS 6.5 or TOEFL 88.
  • Institute of Technology: 60% or 6.0 (on 10) and IELTS 6.5 or TOEFL 79.
  • Colleges: 59% or 5.9 (on 10) and IELTS 6.5 or TOEFL 79.

Universities do not prefer more than 5 backlogs. However, if you have more backlogs, there are always options. Contact us if you want us to assess your profile - it's a free service!

Course Fee for Masters in Biotechnology in Ireland :

Tuition fees for MSc in Biotechnology in Ireland starts from €12,000.

Typical fee range:

Universities: €14,000 to €24,800.

Institutes of Technology and Colleges: €12,000 to €16,000.

Job Options

  • Modern Medicine and Pharmaceuticals - producing antibiotics, creating vaccine
  • Animal Breeding (DNA profiling and cloning)
  • Food Science (producing more nutritious food, and/or genetically modified food)

Salary Options

As of January 2019, average salary of Biotechnology graduate in Ireland is €36,000 per year which is close to 1.5L per month. The salary of a senior Biotechnologist in Ireland nearly € 56,000 – 67,000 per year.
