Institute of Technology Tralee

Accounting Agricultural Engineering Business Information Management Civil Engineering Industrial Instrumentation Lean UX Design Marketing Manufacturing Engineering Master of Business Sports Development Travel & Tourism Wildlife Biology

Established in 1977, Institute of Technology, Tralee is a non-profit public higher education institution located in the medium-sized town of Tralee (population range of 10,000-49,999 inhabitants), County Kerry. Officially accredited and/or recognized by the Department of Education and Skills, Ireland, Institute of Technology, Tralee (ITT) is a small coeducational higher education institution.

Institute of Technology, Tralee (ITT) offers courses and programs leading to officially recognized higher education degrees such as bachelor degrees in several areas of study. This higher education institution does not have a selective admission policy. International applicants are eligible to apply for enrollment.

Why study at IT Tralee?

  • Work placement on all programmes.
  • Work ready graduates with real-world skills.
  • Multicultural campus with almost 15% of students from overseas.
  • In 2013 the IT, Tralee became the first third level institution in the world to receive a UNESCO chair in inclusive physical education, sport, fitness and recreation.
  • Located in Ireland’s most popular tourist region, IT Tralee is surrounded it’s surrounded by great nightlife, restaurants & bars, mountains, rivers, lakes with the wild majestic Atlantic ocean nearby. It’s a stunning place to live.

Educational Requirements

The following entry arrangements will apply to graduates of QQI courses seeking admission to courses at the Institute. The minimum entry requirement for graduates of QQI awards to:

  • Higher Certificate courses is a full QQI award;
  • Ab-initio Ordinary Degree courses is a full QQI award;
  • Ab-initio Honours Degree courses is a full QQI award in a course in a cognate area and including a distinction grade in at least three modules.

Notwithstanding the minimum entry requirements listed above, specific higher certificate and ab-initio ordinary degree courses may have specific prerequisite QQI module or QQI course requirements. Such prerequisite requirements are specified (see Specific Course Requirements). In the case of entry to Honours degree courses, relevant cognate courses and specific prerequisite module requirements, where relevant, are specified (see Specific Course Requirements).

Research Details

Research Programmes are organised through a 'pillar' type structure within the Research Institute whereby defined areas of specialism/potential centres of excellence evolve and support interdisciplinary research programmes. The research strategy being pursued is one which seeks to further strengthen the capacity of the Institute in a manner which will both enhance the educational programme being offered to our students and also enable us to serve the economic, social and cultural life of the region. In addition to the funded research, significant research work is being undertaken by staff members as part of their studies in Masters and PhD programmes.

Some of the exciting Research and Development projects current on campus are.

  • Irish Academy of Hospitality and Tourism
  • The Shannon Applied Biotechnology Centre (Shannon ABC)
  • Intelligent Mechatronics and RFID (IMaR)
  • Centre for Entrepreneurship and Enterprise Development (CEED)
  • UNESCO Chair in Inclusive Physical Education, Sport, Fitness and Recreation.
