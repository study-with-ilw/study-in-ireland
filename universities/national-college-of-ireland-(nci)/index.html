National College of Ireland (NCI)

MSc in Cloud ComputingMSc in CybersecurityMSc in Data AnalyticsMSc in EntrepreneurshipMSc in FinanceMSc in FinTechMA in Human Resource ManagementMSc in International BusinessMSc in ManagementMSc in Marketing

The National College of Ireland is a not-for-profit, State aided higher education institute. It is located in a state of the art campus located in Dublin’s city centre. NCI derives state funding in the form of core grant directly from the Department of Education and Skills.

NCI has strong links with world-renowned US universities. For example, the MSc in Cloud Computing has been developed with professors from Stanford, UC Berkeley and Cornell. The NCI School of Computing is Ireland’s largest and sits in the centre of Dublin which is known as the “Silicon Valley of Europe”. Within walking distance of NCI are the European Headquarters of Google, Facebook, LinkedIn and Twitter. It is with these companies that NCI has developed courses and where NCI graduates gain employment.

The School of Business sits in the centre of Dublin’s International Financial Services Centre. All of the world’s leading banks and consultancy companies are within reach of the campus. NCI graduates have gained employment at great companies like Citi, PWC, Morgan Stanley, KPMG and JP Morgan.

Specialist areas of education covered by the college include human resource management, psychology, management, data analytics, Fintech, cloud computing, cybersecurity, education and accountancy. The college is also known for its public events that are free. These include web technology and dot conf digital conference, the Seven Deadly Skills, marketing Mavericks.

Why study at National College of Ireland?

  • Industry Links – All of NCI’s courses are designed with industry to meet their current and future workforce requirements
  • Employability – 96% of our students are in work or further study six months after graduating
  • Location – Located in the heart of Dublin’s Financial Services Centre and Silicon Docks, surrounded by the world’s biggest and best companies
  • 2 year Post Study Work Visa – All Masters students are eligible for a two-year Graduate Visa
  • Global reach – Partnerships with global educations leaders, including Stanford, UC Berkeley and Cornell

University Ranking

NCI is one of the 18 universities that have been included in the U-Multirank for Ireland. It works in the form of a small private college and a specialized academic institution with regard to the degree programmes and the subjects that it offers. NCI ranking is number one in the field of graduate employment. This ranking has been allotted by the UK Sunday Times University Guide.

Tuition Fees

Postgraduate:

Master’s degree course fee in NCI covers the cost of studies along with charges for tuition, registration, examinations, graduation and supervision. Postgraduate tuition fee in NCI is €14,500.Minimum Tuition Fee for National College of Ireland - €12,835.

Undergraduate:

Bachelors degree course fee in NCI tends to vary as per the courses that are chosen. Bachelors programs tuition fee in NCI is around €10,000.

Educational Requirements

  • Entry Requirement for National College of Ireland (Percentage or CGPA) - 60% or 6.0 CGPA
  • IELTS Requirement for National College of Ireland - 6.0 with no section below 5.5
  • TOEFL Requirement for National College of Ireland - 79
  • PTE Requirement for National College of Ireland - 59.
  • The Undergraduate IELTS requirement in NCI tends to vary from one course to another.

Research Details

Research at National College of Ireland is regarded as a core activity embedded in the academic culture and contributing to the overall mission of the college. Lifelong learning, workplace learning and technology-enhanced learning represent NCI’s historical and current sphere of interest. Within this context, National College of Ireland strives to provide access to quality education at a time, place and pace that meets the learner’s needs.
